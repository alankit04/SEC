Where does the user see the accuracy record?

The ledger is only useful if the advisor sees it at the right moment. Where should RAPHI's track record surface?

A

Inline on every research output

Each memo and signal shows RAPHI's historical accuracy for that ticker and signal type — right below the conviction.


NVDA — Signal View: Positive · MEDIUM conviction
ML: LONG (0.68) · SEC: Accelerating · Apr 8, 2026
RAPHI Track Record on NVDA
ML signals: 72% accurate at 60d (11 calls) · SEC trends: 78% confirmed (9 filings)

Why this works: Context at the exact moment of decision. Advisor sees accuracy before acting on the signal.

Limit: Requires enough historical calls to show meaningful accuracy. Shows nothing for new tickers.

C

Both — inline summary + dedicated page

Every research output shows a compact accuracy line. The full Conviction Ledger page shows aggregate stats, pending lookbacks, regime breakdown, and full history.


On every memo (compact):
RAPHI ML accuracy on NVDA: 72% at 60d · Full ledger →
Ledger page (full):
All tickers · all signal types · regime breakdown · pending queue · full history table

Why this works: Right information at the right moment. Compact inline for quick context. Full page for due diligence.

Build order: Ship the ledger page first (data store + lookback job). Add inline to research outputs once ledger has data.

My recommendation

C — both surfaces, built in order. The ledger page is the data layer. The inline compact view is the UX layer. Build the page first — it forces you to get the data model right. The inline view is two lines of code once the data exists. Together they make RAPHI's track record unavoidable, not optional.
