What counts as a "conviction"?

The Conviction Ledger needs to know what it's tracking. Every entry is a timestamped claim RAPHI makes — that later gets checked against reality. Which outputs should be tracked?

A

ML Signal Only

Track only the quantitative signal direction: LONG / SHORT / HOLD, with raw probability.


📌 Apr 8, 2026 — NVDA — LONG (prob: 0.71)
⏱ Check at: May 8 · Jun 7 · Jul 7
✅ May 8: +8.4% — CONFIRMED

Why this first: Cleanest definition of "proved true" — price went in the signal direction. Fully automatable. No ambiguity.

Limit: Only covers one of the four agent outputs.

B

ML Signal + SEC Trend Verdict

Track the quantitative signal AND the SEC revenue trend call the sec-researcher makes.


📌 Apr 8, 2026 — NVDA
  ML: LONG (0.71) → check vs price at 30/60/90d
  SEC: Revenue accelerating → check vs next 10-Q filing
✅ May 8: ML confirmed · 📋 Jul 15: Next 10-Q pending

Why this: SEC verdict is verifiable — next quarter's XBRL filing either confirms or contradicts "accelerating." Two independent signals, two independent accuracy scores.

Limit: SEC lookback is 90–120 days (filing cadence). Slower feedback loop.

C

All Research Outputs

Track everything RAPHI produces: ML signal, SEC trend, news sentiment call, and the final Signal View (Positive/Negative/Neutral) on every memo.


📌 Apr 8, 2026 — NVDA — Signal View: Positive
  ML: LONG (0.71) · SEC: Accelerating
  Sentiment: Positive (VADER: +0.42)
⏱ 30d · 60d · 90d lookback for each component
📊 Accuracy by component · by regime · by sector

Why this: Maximum insight. Shows which component of RAPHI is most reliable — and when. Enables "trust ML signals more than sentiment in high-VIX environments."

Limit: Most complex to build. "Proved true" for sentiment is harder to define than for price direction.

My recommendation

B — ML Signal + SEC Trend. Gives two independent, verifiable accuracy scores from day one. ML signal is price-checkable in 30 days. SEC verdict is filing-checkable in 90 days. Both are fully automatable with the current data stack. C is the right end-state but A is too narrow and C needs "proved true" definitions that require more design work upfront.
